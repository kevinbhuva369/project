ALL ABOUT THE HINDU TEMPLE

A Hindu temple is a sacred place of worship in Hinduism where devotees pray to different deities such as Shiva, Vishnu, and Durga. It is not only a place for religious rituals but also a spiritual center designed to create peace, concentration, and connection with the divine.

Hindu temples are traditionally built according to ancient architectural principles called Vastu Shastra and Shilpa Shastra, which guide the design, direction, and structure of the temple so that positive energy flows properly. Many temples also have a Mandapa, a hall where devotees gather for prayer, rituals, and religious ceremonies. Hindu temples are important cultural and spiritual centers where festivals, traditions, music, and community activities take place.
