The Sacred Legacy

Shri Hit Radha Keli Kunj is one of the most ancient and revered temples in Vrindavan, intimately associated with the Radhavallabha sampradaya founded by Hita Harivamsa Ji in the 16th century. This sacred path places Srimati Radharani at the absolute pinnacle of devotion as the supreme Goddess, not merely as a consort of Lord Krishna. The temple name itself speaks to its divine nature: "Keli Kunj" means the sacred grove where Radha and Krishna perform their eternal Lila (divine play).

The temple is renowned for the extraordinarily beautiful daily Shringar (adornment) of Shri Radha, who is dressed with seasonal flowers and rare jewellery each day by devoted priests following the ancient tradition of Hita Harivamsa Ji. The inner sanctum exudes a tangible vibration of divine love that has drawn saints, poets, and devotees for five centuries. The legendary poet-saint Swami Haridas guru of the classical musician Tansen is deeply connected to the Vrindavan devotional tradition that this temple represents.

Sacred Architecture

The temple reflects classic Braj-style construction with traditional brick and intricate floral stucco work. The inner sanctum is adorned with silver decorations, fresh seasonal flowers, and handwoven fabrics. The Keli Kunj garden grove within the complex is believed to be the actual spot where Radha and Krishna performed their eternal Raas Leela.

Festivals & Celebrations

Radhashtami the divine birthday of Goddess Radha is the supreme festival here, drawing tens of thousands of devotees from across India. The Holi and Phulera Dooj celebrations are legendary throughout Vrindavan, with flower showers and ecstatic devotional singing continuing through the night. Janmashtami and Jhulanotsav (swing festival) are also celebrated grandly.

The Supremacy of Radha

The Radhavallabha philosophy uniquely places Radha above Krishna in the divine hierarchy. "Radha" literally means "the one whom Krishna worships." This temple embodies that profound theological insight that pure love and total surrender represented by Radha is the highest form of devotion. All rituals here are performed with Radha as the primary focus of worship.

Daily Rituals

The daily schedule begins before dawn with Mangala Aarti, followed by the elaborate Shringar of Shri Radha a ritual that can take over two hours as priests adorn the deity with flowers, silks, and gold in ever-changing seasonal patterns. Multiple Aarti ceremonies punctuate the day, with the evening Sandhya Aarti being especially attended by local Vrindavan residents and saints.

Where Radha is, there is Krishna. Her name precedes His in Radha-Krishna for she is the very essence and embodiment of divine love itself. To reach Krishna, one must first receive Radha's grace. Hita Harivamsa Ji
