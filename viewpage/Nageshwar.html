The Sacred Legacy

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga the 10th of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas is situated on the road connecting Dwarka to Beyt Dwarka on the Gujarat coastline, making it one of the most accessibly located Jyotirlingas. "Nageshwara" means "Lord of the Nagas (divine serpents)" reflecting Shiva's deep symbolic connection to the serpent, which he wears as an ornament, signifying his mastery over the most primordial forces of nature including death and time.

According to the Shiva Purana, a devoted merchant named Supriya was captured by a demon named Daruka and imprisoned with many other devotees. Even in prison, Supriya continued his devoted Shiva puja. Enraged, the demon tried to kill him but Lord Shiva appeared from the earth as a column of blinding light, destroyed the demon, and remained at this site as the Jyotirlinga of Nageshwara. Nearby stands the massive 25-metre Shiva statue that is visible from a great distance along the Gujarat coastline.

Temple Complex

The main temple is relatively modest in size compared to other Jyotirlingas, but the complex includes a large garden and the magnificent 25-metre seated Shiva statue that dominates the landscape. The inner sanctum is underground the devotee descends steps to reach the Shivlinga mirroring the legend of Shiva emerging from beneath the earth. The marble flooring and ornate decorations were added by later benefactors.

Naga Symbolism

Shiva's association with nagas (serpents) is complex and profound in Hindu symbolism. The serpent represents time, death, and the primordial energy of Kundalini. By wearing the serpent as an ornament, Shiva demonstrates his complete mastery over death and time he is the master of that which most terrifies humanity. Nageshwar specifically represents this aspect of Shiva's cosmic dominion.

Sacred Coastal Setting

Located between Dwarka and Beyt Dwarka on the Arabian Sea coast, the drive to Nageshwar passes through beautiful coastal scenery. Many pilgrims combine the Dwarka Char Dham pilgrimage with a visit to Nageshwar, Bet Dwarka, and Rukmini Temple in a single sacred circuit. The combination of sea air, coastal landscapes, and the proximity to Krishna's ancient kingdom creates a uniquely multi-dimensional pilgrimage experience.

The Shiva Purana Legend

The story of Supriya and Daruka at Nageshwar is one of devotion's most poignant narratives faith maintained even in prison, even under threat of death, ultimately invited the divine to physically manifest. Lord Shiva is recorded to have said: "Whoever recites the Nageshwara name, even once, is freed from poison, sorrow, and all evil." This Jyotirlinga specifically represents the protective power of devotion against all adversity.

He who wears time as an ornament, who transforms poison into the jewel that adorns his throat this is Nageshwara, the Lord who demonstrates that there is nothing in creation that is not subject to divine grace. Shiva Purana, Nageshwara Mahatmya
