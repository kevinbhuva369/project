The Sacred Legacy

The Konark Sun Temple is one of the greatest architectural achievements in human history a 13th-century masterpiece built as a giant stone chariot for the Sun God, with 24 intricately carved wheels and seven horses, representing the cosmic journey of time itself.

Built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty in 1250 CE, this UNESCO World Heritage Site showcases the absolute pinnacle of Kalinga architecture. The 24 wheels served as precise sundials, and their shadows could tell the exact time of day.

The Cosmic Chariot

The entire temple is designed as Surya's divine chariot 24 wheels representing 24 fortnights of a year, 7 horses representing 7 days of the week, and the chariot's movement representing the eternal passage of time.

The Sundial Wheels

Each of the 24 massive carved stone wheels functions as a perfect sundial. Trained astronomers can read the time precisely to the minute from the shadow cast by the wheel's spokes.

The Black Pagoda

Konark was historically called the 'Black Pagoda' by sailors, who used it as a navigation landmark. Its magnetic stone reportedly caused compass needles to malfunction.

Konark Dance Festival

The annual Konark Dance Festival (December) is the premier classical Indian dance event held against the backdrop of the temple, with performances by India's greatest classical dancers.

"Konark Sun Temple is India's greatest riddle in stone a mathematical, astronomical, and artistic genius frozen in sandstone for 800 years."
