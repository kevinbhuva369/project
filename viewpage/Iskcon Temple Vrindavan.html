The Sacred Legacy

The ISKCON Krishna-Balarama Mandir in Vrindavan is the jewel of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness worldwide. Built in 1975 by Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada the founder of ISKCON who at the age of 69 single-handedly brought the Hare Krishna movement to the Western world this magnificent white marble temple stands as a monument to his extraordinary vision of making India's temple heritage accessible to the entire world.

The temple uniquely houses six deities on three separate altars: Krishna-Balarama in the centre, Radha-Syamasundara on the left, and Srila Prabhupada's deities (Gaura-Nitai) on the right. Srila Prabhupada himself is entombed in the temple's samadhi (memorial) building, which is decorated with life-size dioramas depicting scenes from his extraordinary life. Devotees from over 120 countries visit this temple every year.

Sacred Architecture

The temple is built entirely in white Makrana marble the same marble used in the Taj Mahal with 50 ornately carved pillars. The three main altars face devotees simultaneously. The surrounding garden, guest facilities, and Prabhupada's Samadhi building form a complete spiritual campus. The evening Aarti with hundreds of devotees chanting creates one of the most powerful devotional atmospheres in Vrindavan.

Festivals & Celebrations

Janmashtami attracts over 100,000 devotees both Indian pilgrims and international visitors. The Holi celebration, called Holi Mahotsav, involves flower showers and ecstatic kirtans for seven consecutive days before the main Holi date. The Gaura Purnima festival (birth of Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu) is celebrated with equal grandeur, drawing thousands of devotees singing through the night.

Global Impact

ISKCON has grown from this temple tradition into a global network of over 650 temples in 120 countries one of the most extraordinary spiritual expansion stories in modern history. Srila Prabhupada's translation of the Bhagavad Gita and Bhagavata Purana into English, published from this tradition, have sold over 500 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling spiritual books of all time.

Daily Schedule

The day begins at 4:30 AM with Mangala Aarti, followed by Tulasi Puja, Guru Puja, and Bhagavatam class. Seven Aarti ceremonies mark the day: Mangala, Sringara, Raj Bhoga, Utthapana, Sandhya, Sayana, and the final Shayana Aarti. Free Prasad is served to all visitors. The ISKCON guesthouse and restaurant make extended spiritual retreats accessible and comfortable.

When I see hundreds of young people dancing and chanting the holy names in the Western countries I know that my spiritual master's mission has been fulfilled. Vrindavan is not a place. It is a state of consciousness. Srila A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada
