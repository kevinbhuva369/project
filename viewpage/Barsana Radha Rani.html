The Sacred Legacy

Barsana is the sacred village where Goddess Radha Rani the supreme beloved of Lord Krishna and the highest embodiment of divine love in the Vaishnava tradition was born and grew up. The Shri Radha Rani temple atop the Barsana hill (240 metres elevation) is the most sacred shrine to Radha in the entire Braj Mandal, built over the divine spot where Radha spent her divine childhood and youth.

The Shri Radha Rani temple, also known as Ladli Ji temple ("beloved daughter"), houses a magnificently adorned idol of Radha Rani in her full divine splendour. The devotional atmosphere at Barsana is unlike any other sacred site in India here, Radha is not a secondary deity but the supreme sovereign, addressed as "Ladli Maharani" (beloved queen). Thousands of devotees from the Radhavallabha and other Vaishnava traditions make annual pilgrimage to pay their respects to Radha in her own homeland.

The Hilltop Temple

The Shri Radha Rani temple is a stunning red sandstone structure atop the Barsana hill, reached by climbing 200 carved steps. The temple complex includes multiple shrines dedicated to Radha's childhood companions (sakhis). The view from the temple of the sacred Braj plains stretching to Nandgaon (Krishna's village) 0 km away is extraordinarily evocative of the divine love story.

Lathmar Holi

Barsana is the starting point of the world-famous Lathmar Holi where women playfully beat men with laths (wooden sticks) while men shield themselves. This unique tradition re-enacts the playful battles between Radha's companions and Krishna's friends during Holi. The event held a week before the actual Holi date draws hundreds of thousands of national and international visitors who witness this uniquely joyful, colourful spectacle.

Braj Mandal Parikrama

The sacred Braj Mandal Parikrama an 84-kroshi (0 km) circumambulation of the entire Braj region visiting 12 forests, 24 groves, and hundreds of sacred sites associated with Radha and Krishna's divine pastimes passes through Barsana as one of its most important stops. This 30-day walking pilgrimage, undertaken by thousands annually, is considered one of the most transformative pilgrimages in the Vaishnava tradition.

Radha's Homeland

Every tree, every stone, every well in Barsana carries the sacred memory of Radha's presence. The four hills of Barsana Brahmagiri, Vishnu, Shiva, and Bana are associated with the four members of the divine household. The Mor Kutir (where Krishna danced as a peacock), Sanket Van, and Pili Pokhar are among the many sacred spots associated with divine pastimes that are venerated with great devotion by Braj pilgrims.

Barsana's very soil is the most sacred treasure in all three worlds for it is the earth that Radha Rani walked upon, that touched her sacred feet, that was honoured by the divine gaze of the supreme goddess of love. To walk here is to walk in paradise. Rupa Goswami, Bhakti Rasamrita Sindhu
