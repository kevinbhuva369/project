The Sacred Legacy

The Jagannath Temple one of the four sacred Char Dham sites is dedicated to Lord Jagannath (a form of VishnuKrishna), his brother Balabhadra, and sister Subhadra. The temple is uniquely distinctive: the wooden idols are replaced every 12 years in a secret midnight ceremony called Nabakalebara.

The 65-metre main tower (Deul) is crowned by a sacred disc (Sudarshana Chakra) and the Patita Pabana flag that can be seen for miles at sea. The temple kitchen (Mahaprasad) cooks for 100,000 people daily.

The Nabakalebara Mystery

Every 12 years, in absolute darkness and secrecy, priests blindfolded with cloth transfer the divine essence (Brahma Padartha) from old wooden idols to newly carved ones one of Hinduism's most mysterious sacred rites.

The Rath Yatra

The annual Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival) is one of the world's largest festivals three massive chariots carrying the deities are pulled by thousands of devotees through 3-km streets. This festival inspired the English word 'Juggernaut'.

The Supreme Mahaprasad

The temple cooks 56 different food items (Chhappan Bhog) for Lord Jagannath daily, using earthen pots stacked on wood fire. This Mahaprasad is considered equal to consuming nectar.

The Mystery of the Flag

The sacred flag atop the temple always flies against the wind direction a phenomenon that defies conventional aerodynamics and has been confirmed by meteorologists.

"At Jagannath's chariot festival, kings become servants and servants become equal to kings. His chariot rolls not on wheels, but on the devotion of all humanity."
