The Sacred Legacy

Baidyanath Dham (also called Vaidyanath Jyotirlinga) in Deoghar, Jharkhand is considered one of the most powerful of all 12 Jyotirlingas. The name "Vaidyanath" means "Lord of Physicians" Lord Shiva is worshipped here in his role as the divine healer who can cure all diseases and relieve all suffering, both physical and spiritual. The temple has 21 subsidiary temples within its campus, making it a complete sacred city in miniature.

The Shravan Mela conducted every July-August at Baidyanath is the largest pilgrimage event in Jharkhand and one of the largest on Earth. Pilgrims known as Kanwariyas walk over 0 km from the Ajgaibinath temple at Sultanganj, carrying sacred Ganga water in decorated bamboo poles (kanwars) on their shoulders during the holy month of Shravan. They cannot put the kanwar down or rest until reaching the Jyotirlinga a feat of extraordinary devotion that brings tens of millions of pilgrims annually.

Sacred Architecture

The main Baidyanath temple is a classic example of Nagara architecture combined with a distinctively Bengali style influenced by centuries of royal patronage. The temple complex houses 21 temples within protective walls, dedicated to various forms of Shiva, Parvati, Kali, and Ganesha. The main Shivlinga is considered so potent that no image or idol is placed beside it it stands in solitary divine power.

The Shravan Kanwar Yatra

The annual Shravan Mela is one of the most dramatic pilgrimages on Earth. Millions of saffron-clad Kanwariyas walk barefoot through the monsoon heat, carrying holy Ganga water for 0 km, not allowed to eat proper meals, sleep on beds, or set down their kanwar. The sight of this vast ocean of devoted humanity moving through the Jharkhand countryside is among the most awe-inspiring expressions of mass faith anywhere in the world.

Vaidyanath The Healer

According to the Shiva Purana, even demon king Ravana prayed here with such intensity that he offered his own 10 heads as sacrifice to Lord Shiva, one by one. Lord Shiva, moved by this devotion, came as "Vaidyanath" (physician) and healed all of Ravana's heads. The linga he granted Ravana is the sacred object of worship here. Devotees believe a sincere prayer to Vaidyanath can cure all diseases that conventional medicine has failed to heal.

Panchakroshi Parikrama

The sacred Panchakroshi Parikrama a 5-kroshi (approximately 0 km) circumambulation of the entire Deoghar sacred zone is performed by dedicated pilgrims who visit all five important shrines in a single prolonged circuit. This parikrama, performed ideally in the month of Shravan, is believed to carry the merit of a complete Jyotirlinga pilgrimage in a single walk.

Where the devotee offers his very life his pride, his ego, his suffering there the divine physician Baidyanath appears, with healing hands and compassionate eyes, making whole what was broken. Shiva Purana
