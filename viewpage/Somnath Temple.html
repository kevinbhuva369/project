The Sacred Legacy

Somnath is the first and foremost of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas sites where Lord Shiva himself manifested as a column of divine light. Standing at the collision of the Hiren, Kapila, and Saraswati rivers at the shores of the Arabian Sea, this temple has been destroyed and rebuilt seven times.

The current magnificent temple was completed in 1951, with the final stones placed by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel. It is built in the Chalukya style using Khandola stone and stands as a symbol of India's indestructible faith.

The Seven Destructions

Somnath was looted and destroyed by Mahmud of Ghazni in 1025 CE, taking away priceless treasures. Yet each time it was destroyed, it was rebuilt by the indomitable spirit of Hindu faith.

The Eternal Lamp

An eternal flame (Akhand Jyoti) has burned in the temple for thousands of years, symbolising the unbroken continuity of divine presence through all destruction and rebirth.

Bhalka Tirth

Near Somnath is Bhalka Tirth the sacred spot where Lord Krishna received the arrow that led to his departure from Earth, completing his earthly mission.

Light & Sound Show

The evening Sound and Light Show depicts the epic history of Somnath its glory, destruction, and eternal revival against the backdrop of the Arabian Sea.

"Even after seven destructions, Somnath rose again. When the divine will is behind creation, no human power can extinguish its flame."
