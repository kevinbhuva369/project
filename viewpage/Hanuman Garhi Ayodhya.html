The Sacred Legacy

Hanuman Garhi the "Fort of Hanuman" is the most important Lord Hanuman temple in Ayodhya and one of the most visited temples in Uttar Pradesh. Located on a hillock in the heart of Ayodhya, the temple is accessed by climbing 76 steep steps a physical act of devotional effort that pilgrims embrace joyfully. Lord Hanuman is traditionally considered the eternal guardian of Ayodhya the city of Lord Rama vigilantly protecting Ram Janmabhoomi (Ram's birthplace) since the Treta Yuga.

The presiding deity is a large idol of Bal Hanuman (infant Hanuman) seated on the lap of his mother Anjana, depicted in a peculiarly tender pose that conveys the divine child's extraordinary power within a frame of innocent sweetness. Tradition holds that Hanuman lived in a cave at this hillock during his earthly sojourn and personally blessed Ayodhya's faithful. Any pilgrimage to Ayodhya is considered incomplete without first seeking Lord Hanuman's blessings at this fort-temple.

Fort Architecture

The temple resembles a small fort on a hill sturdy, compact, and strategically elevated reflecting Hanuman's role as the eternal military guardian of Ram's sacred city. The large courtyard houses multiple smaller shrines. The main sanctum's red-painted walls are decorated with silver and gold ornaments offered by devotees. The view from the top of Hanuman Garhi overlooking Ayodhya and the Sarayu River is magnificent.

Festivals & Celebrations

Hanuman Jayanti (birth of Lord Hanuman, in April-May) is celebrated with extraordinary fervour a massive procession through Ayodhya's streets on this day includes thousands of devotees and the participation of akharas (traditional wrestling communities associated with Hanuman). Ram Navami (birthday of Lord Rama) is also major, as Hanuman considers worshipping on Rama's birthday as his supreme duty.

Guardian of Ram's City

The tradition of visiting Hanuman Garhi before Ram Janmabhoomi reflects a profound devotional protocol one first seeks the blessings of the devoted servant before approaching the master. This reflects the Vaishnava teaching that Hanuman the supreme devotee is the best guide to Ram's grace. No amount of personal spiritual merit substitutes for the blessing of a perfect devotee, which Hanuman represents.

The 76 Steps

The 76 steps are lined with devotees and sadhu alike at all hours. Small shops along the steps sell marigold garlands, sindoor (vermilion Hanuman's sacred offering), and modaks (sweets). The climb itself is devotional each step is a mini-surrender of the ego. At the top, the view of Ayodhya spreading below, with the golden spire of the newly built Ram Mandir visible on the horizon, inspires an extraordinary connection to the sacred ancient landscape.
