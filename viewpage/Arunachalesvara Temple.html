The Sacred Legacy

The Arunachalesvara Temple at Thiruvannamalai is one of India's most anciently sacred sites one of the Pancha Bhuta Stalas representing the fire element, where Shiva manifests as an infinite column of fire (Jyoti). The red Arunachala Hill behind the temple is considered to be Lord Shiva himself in physical form not merely associated with Shiva, but literally his body. To live near, walk around, or gaze upon this Hill is considered equivalent to performing all possible sacred acts.

In the 20th century, this ancient sacred site became the home of Sri Ramana Maharshi one of India's greatest modern sages, who arrived here at age 16 after a spontaneous death-experience that triggered his enlightenment and never left. His 54-year residence at the foot of Arunachala, teaching the path of Self-inquiry (Atma Vichara), drew seekers from across the globe. Today the Ramana Maharshi Ashram adjacent to the temple operates continuously, maintaining his teaching lineage.

Sacred Architecture

The temple is one of the largest in Tamil Nadu spanning 25 acres with four massive Gopurams. The eastern Gopuram at 66 metres is one of the tallest in South India. The temple has 10 sacred halls (mandapas), numerous shrines, and a sacred tank (Brahma Teertha). The complex is so vast that several thousand devotees can be within it simultaneously without overcrowding.

Karthigai Deepam

The Karthigai Deepam festival when a massive beacon fire is lit atop Arunachala Hill is visible from 0 km away and draws over 3 million pilgrims to Thiruvannamalai. This celestial fire represents the original Jyoti Linga (column of fire) of Lord Shiva. Pilgrims perform the 14-km Giri Valam (circumambulation of the hill) on this night under the full moon one of the most electrically spiritual experiences in Indian pilgrimage.

Ramana Maharshi

Ramana Maharshi's teaching of Atma Vichara (Self-inquiry) "Who am I?" is considered by many scholars and practitioners to be the purest and most direct path to self-realisation ever taught in the modern era. His decades of silent presence at this sacred hill transformed hundreds of thousands of lives. Visitors to the Ramana Ashram still powerfully feel the lingering effect of this extraordinary sage's spiritual presence.

Giri Valam

The 14-km Giri Valam (circumambulation of Arunachala Hill) is performed by hundreds of thousands of devotees on every full moon night. Walking barefoot around the hill traditionally done between midnight and dawn is considered the most powerful spiritual practice at Thiruvannamalai, equivalent to performing all other spiritual disciplines. The route passes 8 sacred lingams, each representing one of the 8 directions.
