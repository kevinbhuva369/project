Badrinath

Chamoli, Uttarakhand

The Sacred Legacy

Badrinath is one of the four sacred sites of the Char Dham pilgrimage and one of the 108 Divya Desams the most sacred Vishnu temples. Located at 3,133 metres in the Garhwal Himalayas, it is flanked by the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges with the Neelkantha peak towering behind.

Legend holds that Lord Vishnu performed intense penance at this site for thousands of years, while Goddess Lakshmi protected him from harsh weather by taking the form of a Badri (Indian jujube) tree.

The Sacred Hot Spring

Just outside the temple flows Tapt Kund a natural thermal spring maintaining 45C even in Himalayan winters. Pilgrims bathe here before entering the temple for Darshan.

Adi Shankaracharya's Legacy

Adi Shankaracharya discovered the Vishnu idol in the Alaknanda river and installed it in the temple in the 8th CE, establishing the Char Dham pilgrimage circuit for Hindu unification.

Brahma Kapal Ghat

Brahma Kapal near the temple is the most sacred site in Hinduism for performing ancestral rites (Pitru Tarpan). Offerings made here are believed to directly liberate ancestors.

Maha Abhishek

The annual reopening ceremony (Akshaya Tritiya) includes a grand Maha Abhishek with gangajal, milk, honey, and sacred herbs, attracting tens of thousands of pilgrims.

"In the shadow of the Himalayas, where Vishnu chose to meditate Badrinath speaks not in words, but in the profound silence of eternal snow."
