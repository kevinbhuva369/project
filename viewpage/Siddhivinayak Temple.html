The Sacred Legacy

The Shree Siddhivinayak Ganapati Mandir is Mumbai's most famous temple and one of the wealthiest religious institutions in India. Built in 1801 by Laxman Vithu and Deubai Patil, this magnificent Ganesh temple in Prabhadevi has been a spiritual anchor of Mumbai for over two centuries. The central idol carved from a single black stone is believed to be Swayambhu (self-manifested) and extraordinarily powerful.

The temple is renowned for granting Siddhi the fulfillment of wishes to sincere devotees. Celebrities, politicians, industrialists, and common devotees all stand in the same queue here, reflecting the democratic spirit of divine grace. The temple receives donations exceeding ?100 crore annually and manages extensive charitable and educational programs across Maharashtra.

Sacred Architecture

The temple's current structure was rebuilt in 1991 and features a distinctive golden dome visible from across Prabhadevi. The main idol of Lord Ganesha is unique his trunk is turned to the right (Siddhi Vinayak), which is considered exceptionally rare and auspicious. The inner sanctum is covered in gold and silver offerings from grateful devotees.

Festivals & Celebrations

Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated here with extraordinary grandeur, drawing over 200,000 devotees in a single day. Tuesdays are considered especially auspicious for Lord Ganesha, and the temple sees massive crowds on every Tuesday of the year. The Angarika Chaturthi Ganesh Chaturthi falling on a Tuesday is considered the most powerful day for worship.

Divine Significance

Lord Siddhivinayak is believed to be the Vighnaharta the remover of all obstacles. Devotees come before beginning any new venture a business, a marriage, an exam, a journey seeking the divine blessing to clear their path. The temple's reputation for answering prayers has spread across India and the world, with devotees visiting from dozens of countries.

Daily Rituals

The Kakad Aarti at 5:30 AM begins with Vedic chants and floral offerings. This is followed by Panchamrut Abhishek and multiple puja ceremonies. The Shej Aarti at 9:30 PM is the grand closing ritual, accompanied by temple bells and sacred music. Modak (sweet dumplings) Ganesha's favourite food is the prescribed offering.
