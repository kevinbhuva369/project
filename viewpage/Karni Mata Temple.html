The Sacred Legacy

Karni Mata Temple at Deshnoke is one of the most unique and extraordinary sacred sites in the world a temple where approximately 20,000 rats (called "kabas" sacred souls) are revered as divine, fed from the temple's offerings, and considered the earthly form of Karni Mata's devotees reborn. The rats roaming freely through the marble halls, feeding from the golden bowls of grain and milk, and being carefully protected from harm by the temple's priests and devoted staff create an atmosphere unlike any other temple in India.

Karni Mata (1387-1538 CE) was a Charan (poet-bard caste) woman saint of extraordinary spiritual power, considered by her community to be an incarnation of Goddess Durga. When Yama (the god of death) refused to return the soul of her son (a beloved devotee) from the realm of the dead, Karni Mata made a sacred agreement with Yama that all members of her community would not go to Yama upon death but would instead be reborn as rats in her temple, and from there reincarnate as Charans again. The white rats considered especially auspicious are believed to be Karni Mata herself in manifested form.

The Temple Structure

The white marble temple with its ornate silver-panelled doors (gifted by Maharaja Ganga Singh of Bikaner) is architecturally beautiful a surprise for a temple associated with such an unusual religious tradition. The silver doors depict scenes from Karni Mata's life. The main sanctum houses a life-sized marble idol of Karni Mata surrounded by her sacred rats.

Sighting the White Rat

Among the thousands of resident brown rats, spotting one of the rare white rats is considered an extraordinarily auspicious omen equivalent to receiving a direct blessing from Karni Mata herself. White rats are believed to be avatars of the goddess. Pilgrims sometimes wait for extended periods hoping to glimpse a white rat during their visit.

Festivals

The Navratri festivals (both March-April and September-October) are the principal celebrations, drawing pilgrims from across Rajasthan and beyond. The temple prepares massive quantities of grain, milk, and sweet offerings for both the goddess and the kabas. The entire Deshnoke town transforms into a continuous fair during Navratri.

Karni Mata's Legacy

Karni Mata's prophecies guided the founding of Jodhpur city by Rao Jodha (she granted her blessing for the city's establishment on Mehrangarh Hill in 1459 CE) and was the chosen deity of the Marwar royal family. Multiple Rajput royal houses trace their protection and military victories to her blessings giving her an extraordinary combined spiritual and political legacy across Rajasthan.

In this temple, what the world considers lowly is considered most sacred. What most shun, the devotees embrace with joy. This is Karni Mata's eternal teaching the divine is not where convention says it should be; it is where love has chosen to dwell. Rajasthani Folk Tradition
