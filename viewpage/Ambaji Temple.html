The Sacred Legacy

The Ambaji Temple in Banaskantha district situated in the Aravalli hills near the Gujarat-Rajasthan border is the most important and powerful Shakti shrine in Gujarat and one of the 51 sacred Shakti Peethas. What makes this temple extraordinarily unique is that there is no idol in the inner sanctum instead, a Sriyantra (sacred geometric diagram) inscribed on a gold plate is worshipped as the form of the Goddess. This makes Ambaji one of the rare "formless" Shakti shrines in India.

The Sriyantra is considered the geometric embodiment of the Goddess a mathematical representation of cosmic energy that is considered more potent than any anthropomorphic image. Devotees are not permitted to actually see the Yantra directly it is shielded behind a curtain, and only a brief glimpse is granted during Darshan. The entire temple's energy is organised around this invisible divine centre, creating one of the most mysteriously potent sacred atmoshpheres in all of Gujarat.

Sacred Architecture

The temple's white marble exterior features intricate carvings and the distinctive Gujarat temple style with a prominent Shikhara. The complex is surrounded by beautiful gardens and the Aravalli hills. The Gabbar Hill, 0 km from the main temple where a small shrine marks the actual Shakti Peetha location is accessible by ropeway and offers panoramic views of the sacred landscape.

Navratri Festival

The Navratri celebrations at Ambaji are legendary nine nights of Garba (the traditional Gujarati circular dance) that draw tens of thousands of worshippers in traditional attire. The temple complex and surrounding town transform into a continuous celebration of colour, music, and devotion. The Bhadarvi Poonam (full moon of Bhadra month, September) fair draws over 500,000 pilgrims one of Gujarat's largest gatherings.

The Sacred Yantra

The gold Yantra that is the actual object of worship at Ambaji is said to be self-manifested (not crafted by human hands) and of immeasurable antiquity. The Sriyantra's 9 interlocking triangles represent the 9 forms of Goddess Durga. Each triangle intersection creates a bindu (point) of intense energy concentration. Scientific measurement of the temple area has recorded anomalous electromagnetic field concentrations.

Gabbar Hill Sacred Trek

The Gabbar Hill pilgrimage 0 km from the main temple involves climbing 999 steps to reach the hilltop shrine where Goddess Amba is believed to have physically appeared to her devotees. The view from Gabbar of the surrounding Aravalli valleys and the main temple below is magnificent. Many devotees climb these steps on their knees as an act of supreme devotion.

She who is beyond all form chose to be worshipped as pure geometry the Yantra that encodes the structure of existence itself. In worshipping the Sriyantra, the devotee worships not an image of the divine but the very mathematical logic by which the universe was created. Devi Mahatmya, Markandeya Purana
