The Sacred Legacy

Kanchipuram the "City of Thousand Temples" is one of India's seven sacred Sapta Moksha Puri cities (the seven cities that grant liberation) and one of Tamil Nadu's most historically and spiritually dense sacred sites. For over 2,000 years, Kanchipuram has been a centre of Hindu learning, art, temple architecture, silk weaving, and philosophical discourse. The city is divided into Shiva Kanchi and Vishnu Kanchi, with hundreds of temples dedicated to both traditions.

The three principal temples Ekambareswarar (with a 59-metre Gopuram), Kamakshi Amman (one of the most powerful Shakti shrines in South India), and Varadaraja Perumal (one of the 108 Divya Desams of Vishnu) make Kanchipuram the only city in India to be simultaneously sacred to both the Shaiva and Vaishnava traditions at the highest level. The great philosopher Adi Shankaracharya established one of his four cardinal mathas (monasteries) here the Kanchi Kamakoti Peetham.

Ekambareswarar Temple

Dedicated to Lord Shiva as the Lord of the Mango Tree, the 59-metre Gopuram is one of the tallest in Tamil Nadu. The inner sanctum contains the famous earthen Shivlinga worshipped by Goddess Parvati herself. A 3,500-year-old mango tree inside the complex believed to be the same tree under which Parvati did tapasya still bears fruit today, with each branch producing mangoes of a different variety.

Kamakshi Amman

The Kamakshi Amman temple is one of the three most powerful Shakti shrines in India (along with Meenakshi in Madurai and Visalakshi in Varanasi). The seated golden figure of Kamakshi Devi radiates an extraordinary spiritual energy. The distinctive feature of Kamakshi is that she holds only symbols of grace and prosperity in her four hands unusual for a major Shakti deity, reflecting her supremely benevolent nature.

Silk & Festivals

Kanchipuram's iconic Kanjivaram silk sarees woven with pure mulberry silk and real gold/silver zari have been produced here for over 400 years by weaver communities whose craft is inscribed in the UNESCO Cultural Heritage list. The annual Brahmotsavam festivals at each major temple are spectacular 10-day events. The Kanchipuram Silk Saree Festival in November draws buyers and admirers from across the world.

Philosophical Heritage

Kanchipuram has been home to some of the greatest minds in Indian philosophical history. The Tamil saint Appar, Thirugnana Sambandar, and Manikkavacakar composed sacred hymns here. Ramanuja the great Vaishnava philosopher studied here. The Kanchi Kamakoti Peetham, established by Adi Shankaracharya, continues to be one of the most influential spiritual institutions in South India.

Kanchi is not a city it is a living scripture. Every Gopuram is a chapter, every idol a verse, every stone a word in the eternal poem of devotion that the Tamil saints composed here over two thousand years. Thirugnana Sambandar, Devaram
