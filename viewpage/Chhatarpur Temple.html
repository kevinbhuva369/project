The Sacred Legacy

Chhatarpur Mandir the Shri Adhya Katyayani Shakti Peeth Mandir is the second largest temple complex in India (after Akshardham Delhi) and one of Delhi's most beloved and visited sacred sites. Built in 1974 by Baba Sant Nagpal Ji, this sprawling 70-acre complex of white marble temples dedicated to Goddess Katyayani (the sixth form of Navadurga) rises dramatically at the southern end of Delhi, creating an unexpected vision of sacred splendour amidst the busy capital city.

The complex contains over 20 temples in both North and South Indian architectural styles dedicated to Goddess Durga in various forms, Lord Shiva, Lord Ganesh, Lord Ram and Sita, Lord Hanuman, and other deities. The spectacular Navratri celebrations at Chhatarpur draw over 500,000 devotees over 9 days, making it one of Delhi's largest annual sacred events. The idols of the various deities, particularly the central Katyayani Devi, are exquisitely crafted and lavishly decorated.

Temple Complex

The complex features temples in both Nagara (North Indian) and Dravidian (South Indian) styles a unique blending that makes it an architectural pilgrimage in itself. Special highlights include the large main Durga temple, a beautiful Shiva temple with a large Nandi, a Ram Darbar temple, and the spectacular main gateway complex.

Navratri Celebrations

The 9-day Navratri festival at Chhatarpur is among Delhi's most spectacular elaborately decorated entrances, continuous devotional music, thousands of lamps illuminating every surface, and Jagrata (all-night devotional singing) sessions on multiple nights. Devotees queue for hours for the Devi's darshan during this period of heightened divine presence.

Open to All

Consistent with its founder's vision of universal access to the divine, Chhatarpur Mandir is open to people of all religions, castes, and backgrounds without restriction. Free Prasad (sacred food) is distributed to all visitors daily, and during major festivals the temple provides free langar (community meals) to thousands of pilgrims.

Sant Nagpal Ji

The founder Baba Sant Nagpal Ji was a devotee of extraordinary spiritual intensity whose personal connection to Goddess Katyayani was expressed in every stone of this complex. He chose the southern edge of Delhi's urban sprawl to create a island of sacred peace a gift of spiritual refuge to the citizens of one of the world's most hectic megacities.

In a city that never sleeps, she sits in unwavering stillness. In a capital built on political power, she radiates spiritual power. Katyayani at Chhatarpur is Delhi's most important reminder of what power truly means. Delhi Devotional Heritage
