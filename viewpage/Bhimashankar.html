The Sacred Legacy

Bhimashankar the 6th of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas is an extraordinary temple nestled deep in the Sahyadri mountains at an altitude of 3,250 feet, surrounded by one of Maharashtra's most pristine forest sanctuaries. The Bhima River, one of the major rivers of the Deccan Plateau, originates from the sacred springs at this very site making Bhimashankar not just a Jyotirlinga but the literal source of life for millions downstream who depend on the Bhima River's waters.

According to the Shiva Purana, Lord Shiva slew the demon Tripurasura at this site, and the divine perspiration (sweat) that flowed from Shiva's body after the battle became the Bhima River making the river literally a part of the divine body. This legend gives Bhimashankar an extraordinary sacred geography of cosmic proportions, and the river that flows from this site carries the sanctity of being born from the body of Lord Shiva himself.

Temple Architecture

The Bhimashankar temple is a beautiful example of Nagara architecture with Hemadpanthi construction style (named after Hemadri Pandita, a 13th-century minister who standardized temple architecture across Maharashtra using locally available black stone). The Shikhara (spire) is distinctively wide and heavily adorned. Peshwa rulers of Pune made significant additions to the complex in the 18th century.

Wildlife Sanctuary

The Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary surrounding the temple is home to the Indian Giant Squirrel (Maharashtra's state animal), leopards, sloth bears, and hundreds of bird species. The forest trek to the temple through this extraordinary wilderness is considered one of the most atmospheric pilgrimage approaches in Maharashtra where the natural world and the sacred world converge completely.

Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri draws enormous crowds to Bhimashankar despite its remote forest location. Pilgrims trek through the night, arriving at the temple before dawn by lantern light a pilgrimage experience of extraordinary ancient atmosphere. The monsoon season (June-September) transforms the surrounding hills into lush green paradise, and the waterfalls near the trail make the summer return trek spectacularly beautiful.

The Shivlinga

The Bhimashankar Shivlinga is considered especially potent it is one of the Swayambhu (self-manifested) lingas that appeared of its own divine will rather than being carved or installed by human hands. The linga is described in the Shiva Purana as being "of great size" extraordinarily large compared to other Jyotirlingas. The Panchamrut Abhishek performed at this linga is believed to have healing powers of exceptional intensity.

From Bhimashankar flows the river, from Bhimashankar grows the forest, in Bhimashankar dwells the Lord. Nature here is not separate from the divine it IS the divine, in green and flowing form. Shiva Purana, Maharashtra Khanda
