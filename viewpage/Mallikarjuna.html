The Sacred Legacy

The Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga temple at Srisailam is doubly sacred it is simultaneously one of the 12 Jyotirlingas (Shiva's self-manifested light forms) and one of the 18 Maha Shakti Peethas (where body parts of Goddess Sati fell). This extraordinary dual sanctity makes Srisailam arguably the most spiritually potent site in Andhra Pradesh. The presiding deities are Mallikarjuna (Lord Shiva) and Bhramaramba Devi (Goddess Parvati in the form of a black bee).

The temple is perched on the Nallamala hills above the Krishna River gorge a setting of extraordinary natural beauty and spiritual power. The surrounding Srisailam Tiger Reserve covers 3,568 sq km of dense forest, making the journey to this shrine through pristine wilderness one of India's most atmospheric pilgrimage approaches. Ancient Puranas mention this site as older than memory, with saints and sages having meditated in these forests since the beginning of time.

Sacred Architecture

The temple is a magnificent example of Vijayanagara-era architecture with a massive 50-metre high Gopuram visible across the surrounding hills. The main temple complex houses both the Mallikarjuna Shivlinga and the Bhramaramba Devi shrine within the same campus allowing combined worship of both Shiva and Shakti in a single pilgrimage. The outer walls are covered in intricate sculptures of the divine pantheon.

The Origin Legend

According to the Skanda Purana, Lord Ganesha and Lord Kartikeya competed to marry first whoever circumambulated the universe first would win. Kartikeya rode his peacock to travel the cosmos, while Ganesha simply circumambulated his divine parents (Shiva-Parvati), declaring them to be the entire universe. Shiva and Parvati came to this mountain to bless Ganesha and never returned their divine presence here as Mallikarjuna and Bhramaramba is a permanent manifestation of parental love.

Festivals & Celebrations

Maha Shivaratri is the supreme festival, drawing hundreds of thousands of pilgrims who trek up the hills through the night. The Ugadi (Telugu New Year) celebrations are particularly grand. The temple also observes all 12 monthly Shivaratris with special rituals. The Srisailam Dam, which forms the largest reservoir in South India nearby, adds scenic grandeur to the pilgrimage experience.

Bilva Patra Worship

Bilva (Bael) leaves are the most sacred offering to Lord Mallikarjuna. The Bilva trees in the surrounding forest are considered especially sacred, and pilgrims collect fresh leaves for the Abhishek ceremony. The three-leafed Bilva represents the three Gunas (Sattva, Rajas, Tamas) offering Bilva to Shiva symbolises surrendering all three qualities and their associated ego to the divine.

Where the mountain meets the sacred river, where the gods chose to dwell permanently out of parental love there is Srisailam, where Mallikarjuna and Bhramaramba grant liberation to all who invoke their names. Skanda Purana
