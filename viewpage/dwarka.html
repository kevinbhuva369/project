The Sacred Legacy

The Dwarkadhish Temple Temple of the Lord of Dwarka stands as one of the four Char Dham pilgrimage sites and the holiest shrine dedicated to Lord Krishna. Located in the ancient city of Dwarka on the western tip of Gujarat's Saurashtra Peninsula, this magnificent temple is the spiritual western gateway of India. Lord Krishna himself is said to have established the city of Dwarka as his kingdom after leaving Mathura, ruling here for over a hundred years.

The current temple structure, rising in five stories to 78.3 metres above sea level, was built around the 16th century CE on foundations believed to date back to the time of Lord Krishna himself. The Gomati River flows into the Arabian Sea right beside the temple, creating an extraordinarily sacred confluence. Legend states that the original Dwarka city a magnificent golden city built by the divine architect Vishwakarma was submerged beneath the sea after Lord Krishna departed for his divine abode.

Sacred Architecture

The Dwarkadhish Temple is a masterpiece of the Chalukya architectural style. The main spire rises to 78 metres and is visible from far out at sea. The main hall is supported by 72 ornately carved pillars. The temple complex faces west toward the Arabian Sea and is entered through the sacred Swarga Dwar (Gate of Heaven) and Moksha Dwar (Gate of Liberation).

Festivals & Celebrations

Janmashtami Lord Krishna's birthday is the supreme festival at Dwarka, drawing over 200,000 devotees who celebrate through the night with ecstatic devotional music, dance, and elaborate rituals. Holi is celebrated with extraordinary colours and joy. The Dwarka Utsav in November is a 3-day cultural festival of classical music and dance performances around the sacred temple.

Sacred Geography

Dwarka holds a unique sacred geography it is one of the seven Sapta Puri (seven most sacred cities in Hinduism). Archaeological excavations have found submerged structures up to 0 km offshore, lending credibility to the legend of the sunken golden city. Every year on the night before Janmashtami, the temple's golden flag is changed an extraordinary ritual watched by thousands.

Panchang Darshan

The temple offers five daily Darshans: Mangala (6 AM), Shringar (7 AM12:30 PM), Gwal (5 PM), Sandhya (77:30 PM), and Shayan (8:30 PM). Each Darshan presents the deity in a different divine mood and attire. Devotees traditionally take a holy dip at the Gomati Ghat near the temple and collect the sacred Pancha Dwarka flag stamp as a pilgrimage record.
