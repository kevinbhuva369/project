Dwarkadhish Temple

📍 Dwarka, Gujarat — One of the Sacred Char Dham

The Sacred Legacy

Also known as Jagat Mandir (Temple of the Universe), Dwarkadhish Temple is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India and a cornerstone of the Char Dham circuit. Dedicated to Lord Krishna — worshipped here as the "King of Dwarka" — this magnificent shrine stands as a testament to centuries of unwavering devotion. The city of Dwarka is believed to have been Lord Krishna's kingdom, submerged after his departure, making this temple a deeply sacred connection to his divine lila.

The present temple structure, soaring to a height of approximately 78 metres (256 feet), is believed to have been built over 2,500 years ago, though the existing edifice dates primarily to the 16th century. Constructed in the Chalukya style of architecture, the temple's limestone spire (shikhara) is crowned with a massive flag bearing the sun and moon symbols, visible from miles around. This flag, replaced daily at sunrise, symbolises the eternal presence of Lord Krishna.

The temple is divided into two principal shrines — the Jagat Mandir (main sanctum) and Trivikrama Temple. The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Dwarkadhish — a black stone image of Lord Krishna in his majestic four-armed form. Pilgrims from across India throng to receive the divine blessing of Darshan, particularly during the auspicious festivals of Janmashtami and Holi, when the temple transforms into a breathtaking ocean of devotion and celebration.

Architecturally, the structure rests on 72 pillars and features five floors of intricately carved stone. The main entrance, Moksha Dwar (Gate of Liberation), faces north and leads to Swarg Dwar (Gate to Heaven) on the south bank of Gomti River. Devotees traditionally take a holy dip in the Gomti before entering the temple — a ritual cleansing of the soul before meeting the divine. The temple complex also includes the Kusha Brahma Temple, Devkinandan Temple, and Bhadkeshwar Mahadev Temple, making it a complete spiritual universe.
