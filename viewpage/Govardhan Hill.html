The Sacred Legacy

Govardhan Hill (also called Giri Raj "King of Mountains") is one of the most sacred sites in Braj Mandal, deeply associated with one of Lord Krishna's most famous divine acts lifting the entire hill on his little finger for seven days and nights to shelter the Braj community from the wrathful downpour of rain god Indra. This event, recorded in the Bhagavata Purana, established Govardhan's sanctity as a divine entity in itself not merely a geographical feature but an embodied form of the Lord.

The 21-km Govardhan Parikrama (circumambulation of the hill) is considered one of the most revered pilgrimage rituals in the Vaishnava tradition. Millions of pilgrims some circumambulating the entire 0 km on their knees, some performing full-body prostrations (Dandavat Parikrama) complete this sacred circuit daily. The hill is worshipped directly as Giridhari (bearer of the mountain) one of Krishna's most beloved names and small stones from the hill are considered as sacred as images of the Lord himself.

Mansi Ganga

The sacred Mansi Ganga lake at the foot of the hill is believed to have been created by Lord Krishna from the mental waters of the Ganges (Mansi = mental/imaginative). Bathing in Mansi Ganga is considered equivalent to bathing in the Ganges herself. The lake is surrounded by beautiful ghats and temples, and the morning worship of the rising sun reflected in these sacred waters is one of Braj's most beautiful spiritual sights.

Annakut Festival

Govardhan Puja (Annakut) celebrated the day after Diwali commemorates Krishna lifting this very hill. The entire hill is symbolically worshipped by offering mountains of food (Anna Kuta = mountain of food). The celebrations at Govardhan on this day are among the most spectacular anywhere in Braj with massive quantities of cooked foods piled high as offerings to the sacred hill.

Sacred Sites Along Parikrama

The 21-km Parikrama circuit passes 48 sacred spots including Kusum Sarovar (a beautiful octagonal lake where Radha gathered flowers for Krishna), Radha Kund (most sacred lake in Braj Radha's own bathing lake), Dan Ghati (where Krishna playfully collected taxes from Radha), and the principal shrines of Govardhana Mukharavinda, Chandra Sarovar, and Govind Kund.

The Shila (Sacred Stone)

Small stones from Govardhan Hill are considered manifestations of Lord Krishna himself they are kept and worshipped as primary deities in many Vaishnava homes across India. Each Govardhan Shila has unique natural markings that devotees identify as representing specific forms or attributes of the Lord. Receiving a Govardhan Shila as a sacred gift is considered one of the highest blessings in the tradition.

I, Krishna, am not different from this hill. Govardhan is my very own body offering water, grass, shelter, and love to all living beings unconditionally. To worship Govardhan is to worship me in my most giving, most accessible form. Lord Krishna, Bhagavata Purana 10.24
