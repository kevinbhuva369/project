The Sacred Legacy

Lepakshi is one of the most magnificent and mysterious temples in Andhra Pradesh a masterpiece of Vijayanagara-era art built in 1530 CE by Virupanna Nayaka. The name Lepakshi is derived from a famous episode of the Ramayana when the wounded bird Jatayu, who had valiantly tried to save Sita from Ravana, fell here. Lord Rama comforted the dying bird with the words "Le Pakshi" (Rise, O Bird) making this spot eternally sacred.

The temple is renowned for its extraordinary mural paintings among the finest specimens of Vijayanagara fresco art in the world covering the entire ceiling of the main hall in meticulous detail. The mysteries of Lepakshi include a hanging pillar (one of 70 pillars that appears to hang slightly above the floor a thread can be passed beneath it), the largest monolithic Nandi bull in India (facing the main Shivlinga), and an enormous carved footprint believed to be that of Goddess Sita.

The Hanging Pillar

Among the temple's 70 pillars, one famously "hangs" suspended slightly above the floor. Visitors can pass a cloth under it to verify this. The structural secret has puzzled engineers for centuries. British engineers in 1910 tried to move it to understand the construction and slightly displaced it causing other pillars to tremble, so the experiment was abandoned.

Vijayanagara Murals

The ceiling of the Kalyana Mandapa (marriage hall) contains the world's largest Vijayanagara-era fresco over 70 feet long depicting the divine wedding of Shiva and Parvati with hundreds of divine figures. The pigments, made from natural minerals, remain vibrant after 500 years with no chemical restoration.

The Giant Nandi

The monolithic Nandi bull at the entrance 27 feet long, 15 feet tall, carved from a single granite boulder is the largest monolithic Nandi in India. The detail of the jewellery, facial expression, and muscular form represents the absolute pinnacle of Indian stone-carving in the Vijayanagara tradition.

Veerabhadra

The main deity Veerabhadra is a fierce warrior form of Shiva, created by Shiva to avenge the death of his wife Sati at Daksha's yajna. The 11-foot idol stands in full battle armour and is considered extraordinarily powerful for protection against all evil and injustice.

Rise, O Bird even in your final moment, you have demonstrated the highest devotion: giving everything you have in service of what is right, regardless of the cost. Lepakshi is the eternal monument to that sacred courage. Lord Rama to Jatayu, Valmiki Ramayana
