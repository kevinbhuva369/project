World's Largest ISKCON Temple

The Sacred Legacy

The ISKCON Sri Radha Krishnachandra Temple in Bangalore is the world's largest ISKCON temple an extraordinary white marble complex spread over 7 acres in the heart of the city. Inaugurated in 1997, it was built by Madhu Pandit Dasa and draws over 10,000 visitors daily, making it one of Karnataka's most visited attractions. The main temple hall can seat 10,000 devotees simultaneously for the spectacular evening Aarti.

The Bangalore ISKCON is particularly famous for its mid-day Prasad distribution serving over 150,000 hot, nutritious meals daily to schoolchildren across Karnataka through its acclaimed Akshaya Patra Foundation the world's largest NGO school lunch program, operating in 19 states and feeding 2 million children every school day. This extraordinary combination of devotional excellence and social service represents the temple's unique contemporary expression of Bhakti yoga.

Architecture

The complex features white marble towers, a stunning central hall with painted ceilings depicting Krishna's pastimes, a Vedic museum, a gift shop, a restaurant serving vegetarian prasad, and extensive landscaped gardens. The 7-storey Vimanagiri tower houses the main deity.

Akshaya Patra

The Akshaya Patra Foundation born from this temple's kitchen serves 2.2 million children across India daily with hot mid-day school meals, reducing dropout rates and eliminating hunger. This is the largest such program in the world by any NGO.

Janmashtami

The annual Janmashtami celebration draws over 200,000 visitors in a single day the entire complex illuminated with devotional displays, classical dance performances, and midnight Abhishek of Lord Krishna. The event is telecast live to devotees worldwide.

Daily Schedule

Seven Aarti ceremonies daily beginning at 4:15 AM. Free Prasad served to all visitors at specific timings. Vedic cultural shows, exhibitions, and spiritual discourse sessions run throughout the day for visitors and devotees.
