The Sacred Legacy

The Akshardham temple complex in Gandhinagar is the original Akshardham built by the Bochasanwasi Akshar Purushottam Sanstha (BAPS) in 1992, preceding the more famous Delhi Akshardham by 13 years. Constructed entirely from 6,000 tonnes of Rajasthani pink sandstone (without steel or concrete) in just 18 months by 1,200 artisans, this magnificent temple stands as a testament to the enduring vitality of India's sacred construction traditions.

The temple sits on the banks of the Sabarmati River adjacent to the Gujarat state capital's lake, creating an extraordinary visual and spiritual setting. Dedicated to Swami Sahajanand Swami (Swaminarayan), the Akshardham philosophy emphasises devotion, ethics, and community service as inseparable aspects of spiritual life. The temple campus includes extensive educational exhibitions, a musical fountain show, and beautifully landscaped gardens.

Pink Sandstone Marvel

The main temple, built from Rajasthani pink sandstone with no steel reinforcement, features over 6,000 sculptures of gods, saints, and mythological scenes carved by master craftsmen. The 23 carved domes of varying sizes are each uniquely decorated. The central shrine houses a gold-plated Swaminarayan idol. The stone-on-stone interlocking construction is expected to last 10,000 years.

Cultural Exhibitions

The campus features Sahajanand Van (a beautiful garden with 87 bronze statues), Neelkanth Varni Abhishek (interactive circumambulatory exhibition), and the Satyam Shivam Sundaram exhibition hall. Evening water show (Sahaj Anand Water Show) combines fountains, lights, fire, and water in a spectacular 24-minute multimedia spiritual performance that retells stories from the Srimad Bhagavatam.

Swaminarayan Philosophy

Lord Swaminarayan (1781-1830) preached the inseparable unity of devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jnana), austere living (vairagya), and righteous conduct (dharma). He also led progressive social reforms opposing sati, widow mistreatment, and substance abuse centuries before such causes gained widespread attention. Today BAPS operates 3,300 temples and hundreds of charitable hospitals and schools worldwide.

Daily Puja Schedule

The temple conducts five daily puja ceremonies each presenting the presiding idol in traditional Swaminarayan tradition: Mangala, Shringar, Raj Bhog, Sandhya, and Shayana. Satsang (spiritual discourse) sessions are conducted regularly for devotees. The Friday evening Aarti is particularly attended by thousands of Gujarati devotees from across Gandhinagar and Ahmedabad.

The purpose of this temple is not to impress it is to transform. Every stone carved here is a meditation. Every courtyard entered is a step inward. The divine that resides here resides equally within you the temple simply makes that truth apparent. Pramukh Swami Maharaj, BAPS Founder
