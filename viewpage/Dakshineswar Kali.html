The Sacred Legacy

The Dakshineswar Kali Temple on the banks of the Ganges near Kolkata is one of the most historically and spiritually significant temples in modern India inextricably linked to the life, visions, and transformative experiences of the great mystic Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa. Built in 1855 by the philanthropist Rani Rashmoni, the temple became the stage upon which one of the most extraordinary spiritual lives in human history was lived out in full public view.

Sri Ramakrishna served as the temple's main priest for many years, during which he experienced extraordinary divine visions of Goddess Kali as a living, breathing, laughing mother rather than a stone idol. His spiritual odyssey practicing virtually every religion (Hinduism, Islam, Christianity) to their ultimate conclusion and finding the same divine truth in all of them, laid the philosophical foundation for the universal Vedanta movement that would later be carried worldwide by his disciple Swami Vivekananda.

Sacred Architecture

The nine-domed Bengali architecture of the main Kali temple is surrounded by 12 Shiva temples arranged along the Ganges bank and a Radha-Krishna temple. The complex is laid out on 25 acres and features beautiful gardens, bathing ghats on the Ganges, and multiple smaller shrines. The main idol of Goddess Bhavatarini stands on a silver lotus on the chest of Lord Shiva.

Festivals & Celebrations

Kali Puja (coinciding with Diwali) is the supreme festival here, when the temple and entire Ganges bank are illuminated with thousands of lamps creating a breathtaking river of light. Durga Puja Bengal's greatest festival is celebrated with extraordinary devotion. The birthday of Sri Ramakrishna in February draws thousands of spiritual seekers from across India and the world.

Ramakrishna's Visions

Sri Ramakrishna's extraordinary spiritual experiences at this temple including direct conversations with the Goddess Kali, visions of the divine forms of all major religions' deities, and extended states of samadhi (spiritual ecstasy) that lasted for hours are documented in The Gospel of Sri Ramakrishna, one of the most extraordinary spiritual biographies ever written, translated into dozens of languages worldwide.

Temple & River Rituals

The daily ritual schedule includes Mangala Aarti at 6 AM and Sandhya Aarti at sunset both conducted with elaborate lamp waving and devotional singing that echoes across the Ganges. Devotees traditionally take a dip in the Ganges at the temple ghats before entering. Prasad from the temple cooked in the sacred kitchen is distributed freely to all visitors regardless of background.

She is the Divine Mother she who gives birth, sustains, and ultimately receives back all of creation. In her terrible aspect she destroys the ego; in her gentle aspect she nurtures the soul. She is all. Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa
