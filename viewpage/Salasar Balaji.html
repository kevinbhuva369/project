The Sacred Legacy

Salasar Balaji is one of the most famous and revered Hanuman temples in North India unique in presenting Lord Hanuman with both a beard and moustache, an unusual iconographic form found almost nowhere else in India. The temple was established in 1754 CE when a miraculous self-manifested Hanuman idol was discovered in a farmer's field at Asota village and, following divine guidance received in a dream, was brought to Salasar by Mohandas Ji Maharaj a Hanuman devotee of extraordinary spiritual purity.

The Salasar temple draws millions of devotees annually from across Rajasthan, Haryana, Delhi, and UP. The temple is especially famous for fulfilling wishes related to marriage, progeny, court cases, and business success. The Saturday and Tuesday crowds are particularly massive, with devotees arriving from distances of hundreds of kilometers to seek the Balaji's blessings. A gigantic Dharamshala complex accommodating thousands of pilgrims simultaneously has grown around the temple.

The Sacred Idol

The bearded, moustached form of Balaji at Salasar is considered Swayambhu (self-manifested). The idol's distinctive features with a large beard and moustache unlike any other Hanuman idol are explained by devotees as reflecting Hanuman's form when he appeared in his most powerful warrior aspect. The idol is adorned with gold and silver ornaments donated by grateful devotees.

Chaitra & Ashwin Purnima

The two annual fairs during Chaitra Purnima (March-April) and Ashwin Purnima (September-October) are among Rajasthan's largest drawing over 500,000 pilgrims each. During these fairs, the roads for 0 km around Salasar are packed with buses, vehicles, and walking devotees in a continuous river of orange (Hanuman's sacred colour).

Miracle Stories

The oral tradition of Salasar Balaji is rich with miracle accounts devotees healed of terminal illness, court cases won after impossible odds, marriages arranged after years of failure. These accounts form a living oral mythology that continuously replenishes the temple's reputation as a wish-fulfilling sacred site of extraordinary potency.

Temple Complex

The growing complex now includes the main Balaji temple, a Shivan temple, a Govind Dev Ji temple, and extensive free accommodation facilities (Dharamshala) for pilgrims. The temple kitchen runs continuously, providing free meals to pilgrims throughout major festival periods a tradition of sacred hospitality that the temple's managing trust has maintained since its founding.

One who takes refuge in Hanuman is never defeated, never abandoned, never without protection. The bearded warrior of Salasar is the embodiment of this promise ancient, powerful, and eternally watchful over those who call upon him. Rajasthani Devotional Tradition
