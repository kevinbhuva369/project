The Sacred Legacy

Trijuginarayan is an ancient Himalayan temple said to be the exact location of the divine wedding of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, presided over by Lord Vishnu himself. The sacred fire that burned at this divine wedding is believed to have been burning continuously for three yugas (cosmic ages).

Located at 1,980 metres in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, this pristine and remote temple is one of the least-known yet most spiritually significant sites in all of Hinduism.

The Eternal Sacred Fire

The most extraordinary feature of Trijuginarayan is the Akhand Dhuni an eternal flame burning continuously since the divine wedding of Shiva and Parvati. Pilgrims add wood to keep this fire alive as an act of devotion.

The Three Sacred Ponds

Three sacred ponds Brahma Kund, Vishnu Kund, and Rudra Kund flank the temple. Their water is believed to cleanse all sins and grant the blessings of the divine trinity.

The Divine Wedding Story

According to Puranas, Parvati performed intense tapasya in these mountains to win Shiva's love. Vishnu gave her in marriage to Shiva here a cosmic event whose fire still burns in the temple.

Pilgrimage Circuit

Trijuginarayan is part of the Kedarnath pilgrimage circuit just 0 km from Kedarnath. Pilgrims often combine both sites in a single sacred journey through the Himalayas.

"To witness flame burning since the wedding of Shiva and Parvati is to touch time itself to stand at the meeting point of the mythological and the eternal."
