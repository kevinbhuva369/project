The Sacred Legacy

The Harmandir Sahib the Golden Temple is the spiritual and cultural centre of the Sikh faith and one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in the world. Built by Guru Arjan Dev Ji in 1604 CE and later covered in 24-carat gold by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, it stands as a beacon of equality and seva.

The temple is deliberately constructed below ground level symbolising that humility is the first step toward divinity. Its four entrances face four directions, welcoming people of all faiths, castes, and backgrounds.

The Amrit Sarovar

The sacred pool (Sarovar) surrounding the temple means 'Pool of the Nectar of Immortality'. Pilgrims take a holy dip believing it cures illness and cleanses the soul.

24 Hours Kirtan

Sacred Gurbani Kirtan is sung continuously 24 hours a day inside the inner sanctum making the Golden Temple perhaps the only place on Earth with constant divine music.

Langar Free Kitchen

The Langar (community kitchen) serves free vegetarian meals to over 1,00,000 people daily regardless of religion, caste, or social status the greatest ongoing act of service in the world.

Baisakhi Celebration

Baisakhi (April 13-14) marks the founding of the Khalsa and is celebrated with immense devotion the temple glows like the sun and millions participate in the celebrations.

"The Golden Temple does not just glitter with gold it shines with the light of equality, service, and the love of the Formless Divine."
