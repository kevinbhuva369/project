The Sacred Legacy

Shri Krishna Janmabhoomi the birthplace of Lord Krishna is one of the most emotionally powerful pilgrimage sites in the Hindu world. Located in Mathura, one of India's seven sacred Sapta Puri cities, this site marks the precise spot where, according to Puranic tradition, Lord Krishna was born at midnight on Ashtami of Krishna Paksha (the dark fortnight) of the month of Bhadrapada an event that transformed the destiny of the entire universe.

The prison cell (Garbhagriha) where King Kansa imprisoned Devaki and Vasudeva, and where the divine child Krishna appeared, is preserved as the holiest spot. The main shrine complex contains a beautiful temple built over the actual birth site, with an adjacent cell that contains the exact spot marked with a footprint of the divine child. The atmosphere of this site carries an extraordinary emotional charge devotees often weep spontaneously upon entering the inner sanctum.

Sacred Complex

The complex contains the main Keshavdeva Temple, the Garbhagriha (birth cell), and multiple other shrines across a large sacred campus. The main temple was built and rebuilt multiple times over centuries. The current structure, built in 1965 by the prominent industrialist Jugal Kishore Birla, stands adjacent to a 17th-century mosque creating a unique coexistence of two faiths at this extraordinary historic site.

Janmashtami

The Janmashtami celebrations at Mathura are among the most spectacular in India the entire city transforms into a festival ground with illuminations, processions, and devotional programmes. At midnight on Janmashtami, the moment of divine birth is re-enacted with the ringing of bells, blowing of conches, and the bathing of a small Krishna idol. The entire region from Mathura to Vrindavan (0 km) becomes one continuous festival for 8 days.

The Divine Birth

According to Bhagavata Purana, at the precise midnight moment of Krishna's birth, all prison locks burst open, all guards fell into deep sleep, and Vasudeva was guided by divine light to carry the infant across the flooded Yamuna river to the safety of Gokul. The thunderstorm that accompanied the birth parted as the divine infant held out his hand. Mathura's very geography seems shaped by this extraordinary cosmic event.

Mathura Parikrama

The sacred Mathura Parikrama (circumambulation of the city) is a 14-km walk around the city that passes all major sacred sites: the Janmabhoomi, Vishram Ghat (where Krishna rested after killing Kansa), Dwarkadhish Temple, Yamuna River ghats, and 25 important temples. Completing this circuit is considered a supreme act of devotion the equivalent of a complete Char Dham pilgrimage according to some scriptures.

On that night of cosmic transformation, the prison became the birthplace of liberation. The very chains that bound the divine parents dissolved for when God is born, all bondage must yield. Mathura holds this moment eternally in its sacred soil. Bhagavata Purana
