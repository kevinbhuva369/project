Jakhu Temple

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh

The Sacred Legacy

The Jakhu Temple is the most visited temple in Shimla, perched at the highest peak of the Shimla hills at 2,455 metres. Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, it is believed to be the exact spot where Hanuman rested while searching for the Sanjeevani herb to save Lakshmana's life.

The temple is famous for its giant 108-feet statue of Lord Hanuman a landmark visible from almost every point in Shimla. Climbing the 2-km trail through cedar forests and playing monkeys is itself a divine experience.

The Ramayana Connection

Jakhu Peak is believed to be where Hanuman paused during his search for the life-saving Sanjeevani herb. The footprint of Hanuman is allegedly preserved within the temple premises.

The 108-foot Hanuman

The massive 108-foot Hanuman statue erected in 2010 is an engineering marvel and a spiritual beacon. At night, lit up against the Himalayan backdrop, it creates an awe-inspiring sight.

The Resident Monkeys

Hundreds of rhesus macaque monkeys live around and inside the temple premises, considered sacred as descendants of Hanuman's monkey army. They participate freely in the temple's daily life.

Panoramic Views

The temple offers 360-degree views of the Shivalik ranges, Shimla's colonial rooftops, and on clear days the snow-capped Great Himalayan peaks. Few temple views in India match this.

"At Jakhu, Lord Hanuman's presence is not in the idol it is in the wind in the cedar trees, the clouds brushing the peak, and the devotion in every pilgrim's eye."
