The Sacred Legacy

The Ram Mandir at Ayodhya is one of the most significant temples in modern Indian history, standing on the sacred site believed to be the birthplace of Lord Ram seventh avatar of Vishnu and the ideal king of Treta Yuga.

Built in magnificent Nagara style using pink sandstone from Rajasthan, the temple is 380 feet long, 250 feet wide and 161 feet tall, with 392 pillars and 44 doors adorned with divine sculptures.

Nagara Grandeur

The temple's three-storey Nagara architecture features intricate carvings of the Ramayana story across every pillar and wall, making it a visual scripture in stone.

Garbhagriha (Sanctum)

The inner sanctum houses the idol of Shri Ramlalla the child form of Lord Ram sitting on a golden throne, adorned with royal garments and precious jewels daily.

Sacred Site History

Ayodhya is one of the seven most sacred cities (Saptapuri) of Hinduism. The site's sanctity is mentioned in the Ramayana, Puranas, and countless ancient texts.

Grand Complex

The 70-acre campus includes museums, pilgrimage facilities, meditation gardens, and facilities for lakhs of pilgrims visiting from across the world.

"Wherever there is dharma, there is victory. The Ram Mandir stands as an eternal symbol of faith, patience, and the triumph of righteousness."
