The Sacred Legacy

Kedarnath one of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas is India's most dramatically situated temple. Perched at 3,583 metres in the Garhwal Himalayas, surrounded by snow-capped peaks, this ancient shrine to Lord Shiva embodies the raw, untamed energy of the divine in nature. The temple stands as a testament to human devotion transcending all physical limits.

The current temple structure was built by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century CE. The Pandavas are said to have sought Lord Shiva's forgiveness here after the Kurukshetra war. Lord Shiva, in the form of a bull, dived into the earth here different body parts emerging at the five Panch Kedar shrines across the Garhwal Himalayas.

Sacred Architecture

The rectangular temple with conical Shikhara has survived centuries of Himalayan winters and the catastrophic 2013 flood that destroyed the surrounding town but left the temple miraculously intact.

Festivals & Celebrations

The temple opens on Akshaya Tritiya (April-May) with elaborate ceremonies. Maha Shivaratri is celebrated with great devotion. The doors close on Bhaishnav Chaturdashi when a divine lamp is lit and burns continuously through the six winter months.

Jyotirlinga Significance

As one of the 12 Jyotirlingas, Kedarnath carries extraordinary spiritual power. The main Shivlinga is in the form of a triangular hump â€” the divine form of Lord Shiva who appeared here as a bull. Ancient scriptures state that a sincere darshan here grants liberation.

The Sacred Trek

The 16-km trek from Gaurikund through the Kedar Valley is itself a spiritual purification. Pilgrims walk through forests, mountain streams, and alpine meadows. Helicopter services from Phata, Guptkashi, or Sitapur also available.
